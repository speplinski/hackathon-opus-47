Mid-lesson energy paywall — validated direction.

1 · Executive summary

One cluster, twenty-seven Duolingo reviews, six design lenses, two iterations. Streak loss reframed as an architectural failure of monetisation timing.

2 · User pain signal

Mid-lesson energy depletion blocks task completion. Loss-framed exit, pre-checked Super, no resume affordance.

3 · Measured pain spaces

Seven heuristics, severity sum 55. Every defect grounded in actual product screenshots — not inferred from prose.

4 · Priority reasoning

Five-dimensional weighted score. Cluster 11 lands first by frequency × severity × business-model misalignment.

5 · Validated direction

Auto-save mid-lesson. Streak protected by default. Monetisation moved to lesson-complete boundary.

6 · Out-of-baseline observations

Pricing inconsistency surfaced by Opus 4.7 — Super is 500 gems on the energy screen, 450 gems on the paywall modal. Same product, two prices, same session.

7 · Audit trail

Two iterations. Zero rejected. Severity 55 → 3, a 95% reduction. Every iteration recorded — accepted and rejected alike.

8 · Signal quality indicators

Coverage component, agreement component, residual component — reported separately. Designers see what the model was certain of and where it stretched.

9 · Handoff notes

What the system commits to: typed inputs, append-only log, every claim traceable. What it does not: ship code, merge, replace human judgment.

10 · Provenance

SHA-256 of every input file: corpus, screenshots, skill prompts, model responses. Cryptographic chain from raw user sentence to final recommendation.
